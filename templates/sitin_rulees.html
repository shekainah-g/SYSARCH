Sit-In Rules and Regulations

Sit-in privileges are granted to students for academic-related activities. To ensure fair usage, safety, and proper laboratory management, all sit-in users must strictly observe the following rules and regulations:

  1. Sit-in users must register properly and secure approval from the laboratory personnel before using any computer unit.
  2. Sit-in is allowed only during vacant laboratory schedules and is subject to availability of computer units.
  3. Priority shall always be given to classes, laboratory sessions, and officially scheduled academic activities.
  4. Sit-in privileges are intended strictly for academic purposes such as:
    • Research and school-related activities
    • Programming and system development
    • Project and assignment completion
  5. Accessing non-academic, illegal, pornographic, or offensive websites is strictly prohibited.
  6. Playing games, watching movies, streaming, or using social media unrelated to academic work is not allowed.
  7. Downloading, installing, deleting files, or modifying computer settings without permission from laboratory personnel is prohibited.
  8. Sit-in users must observe proper decorum and maintain silence inside the laboratory. Acts that may disturb other users are not tolerated.
  9. Food, drinks, chewing gum, smoking, and any form of vandalism are strictly prohibited inside the laboratory.
  10. Personal belongings such as bags and backpacks must be placed in the designated area as instructed by the laboratory personnel.
  11. Sit-in users must log out, close all programs, and properly shut down the computer after use.
  12. Any technical problem or system malfunction must be reported immediately to the laboratory supervisor or student assistant.
  13. Failure to comply with these rules may result in suspension or revocation of sit-in privileges.
DISCIPLINARY ACTION

• First Offense – Verbal warning and possible suspension of sit-in privileges.

• Second Offense – Temporary revocation of sit-in privileges and endorsement to the Guidance Office.

• Serious or Repeated Offenses – Permanent disqualification from sit-in privileges and possible disciplinary action per university policy.
